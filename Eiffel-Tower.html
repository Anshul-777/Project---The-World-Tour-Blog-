Eiffel Tower: The Iron Lady of Paris

By Rahul Sharma April 11, 2025 9 min read

The Eiffel Tower, or La Tour Eiffel in French, stands as the most iconic symbol of Paris and one of the most recognized landmarks in the world. Located on the Champ de Mars near the Seine River, this towering iron structure draws millions of visitors every year.

Historical Significance

The Eiffel Tower was constructed between 1887 and 1889 as the entrance arch to the 1889 Exposition Universelle (World's Fair), held to celebrate the 100th anniversary of the French Revolution. Designed by engineer Gustave Eiffel, the tower was initially met with criticism but has since become a beloved emblem of French art, culture, and ingenuity.

Standing 330 meters (1,083 feet) tall, the tower was the tallest man-made structure in the world until the completion of the Chrysler Building in New York in 1930.

Architectural Marvel

The Eiffel Tower was built using over 18,000 individual iron parts and 2.5 million rivets. Its open-lattice design not only gives it aesthetic charm but also provides immense structural strength. The tower has three levels accessible to the public, offering panoramic views of Paris from every angle.

Key highlights include:

  • The First Floor: Features a glass floor, restaurants, and exhibits
  • The Second Floor: Home to the famous "Le Jules Verne" restaurant
  • The Summit: Offers breathtaking 360-degree views of the city

Visiting the Eiffel Tower Today

Whether by stairs or elevator, ascending the Eiffel Tower is a must-do for any traveler in Paris. The tower is beautifully lit each night, and every hour after sunset, it sparkles for five magical minutes. Seasonal events and exhibitions are frequently held within its structure.

The tower is open year-round, though spring and autumn offer the best weather and lighter crowds. Booking tickets online in advance is highly recommended to skip long lines.

"The Eiffel Tower isn't just a landmark — it's a love letter in iron, written to the skies of Paris." – Local Parisian Guide

Personal Experience

During my visit to the Eiffel Tower, I was stunned by the architectural elegance and the views from the top. Watching the sun set over the Seine while the city lights began to glow was an unforgettable experience. The sparkling tower at night felt straight out of a dream.

Enjoying a croissant beneath the tower, surrounded by artists and the sound of street music, truly captured the soul of Paris for me.

Travel Tips

  • Buy your tickets online to avoid queues
  • Visit early morning or late evening for fewer crowds
  • Carry a camera — the views are spectacular
  • Bring a jacket if visiting the summit — it's windy!
  • Stay after sunset to see the hourly sparkle

The Eiffel Tower continues to inspire awe and romance more than a century after its construction. Whether you admire it from afar or climb to the top, this Parisian jewel is a must-see for every traveler exploring the world.
